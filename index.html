John Mayer

John Clayton Mayer is a famous American pop, rock, blues singer-songwriter and record producer. He has sold over 20 million copies of his albums worldwide, including multiple multi-platinum records. John Mayer has also won several awards for his music, including grammy awards for Best Male Pop Vocal Performance and Best Pop Vocal Album. Below are a few more intersting facts about John Mayer's career.

  • Born in Bridgeport, Connecticut on October 16, 1977, enrolled in Berklee College of Music, but dropped out and moved to Atlanta, Georgia to pursue his musical interests.
  • Signed to a record deal after his performance at the South by Southwest festival caught the eye of people from Aware Records
  • His first two albums: Room for Squares(2001) and Heavier Things(2003) achieved multi-platinum status.
  • Has won multiple grammy awards for Best Pop Vocal Album and Best Male Pop Vocal Performance
  • His secondary career pursuits in the entertainment industry include: television hosting, comedy, writing, and authoring columns for magazines such as Esquire.
  • Has performed with several notable blues artists including: B.B. King, Buddy Guy, and Eric Clapton
